Coordinated paraphrase campaigns flood regulatory public-comment dockets at million-comment scale, and FOIA records enumerate only a small fraction of the actual coordination. Multiple testing over adaptively-discovered candidate clusters must control false-discovery rate while detecting coordination the labels do not record. We construct a cluster-level e-value via within-cluster sample-splitting under a von Mises-Fisher template-paraphrase alternative, with the corpus-marginal null estimated as a movMF mixture on cluster-disjoint SBERT-embedded singletons. The estimator is finite-sample valid without regularity conditions on the alternative. Marginal mis-specification inflates the FDR bound per held-out observation, so we fit on the broad singleton pool where sphere coverage is defensible. Individual validity lets conditional-calibration boosting (Lee, Ren, 2024) and closed-eBH (Xu, Fischer, Ramdas, 2025) compose strictly. Under a vMF alternative we prove the e-value exceeds $K/\alpha$ with probability $\ge 1-\beta$ whenever $\kappa \ge C\cdot\max(d/\sqrt m, \sqrt{d\log(K/\alpha)/m})$, with $C \in [2,4]$ empirical. The smallest docket clusters fall outside this guarantee. Applied at $\alpha=0.10$ to the FCC 2017 "Restoring Internet Freedom" docket (15,748 size-$\ge$-8 Leiden-CPM clusters), the procedure surfaces 8,494 unattributed coordinated clusters whose texts match independent pro-net-neutrality form-letter signatures 91% of the time. It also converges with the 2021 NY Attorney General attribution to 6,305 paid-astroturf clusters, itself a text-forensics derivative. Against those labels precision rises from the cohesion baseline's 29.2% to 38.6% at matched recall, but the 91% rate suggests this is a labeling floor rather than the true coordination rate.
